Minimising negativity

= Strategy of decreasing the amount of negativity in your life

Contentness score

= Measure of minimising negativity;
  • i.e. a measure of how much negativity is impacting your life
  • Higher contentness score = less negativity (good);
  • Lower contentness score = more negativity (bad)
  • We considered 'Negativity score' but felt that the double negative of higher score being more negative (so higher score = bad) was too confusing.

Where the concept comes from

It is a concept formed from thinking backwards;
  • Regrets, and how many old, wise people wish they would have lived their lives...
  • And what many people ultimately strive for after gaining that wisdom.

What a low negativity score looks like

With a low negativity score (good), you will never complain. Never feel down.
  • Small moments of joy in life will turn big.
  • You will ironically be able to gain great pleasure from the true lasting sources, such as helping others, and feeling like you are important to society.
  • Every day you will look back on the previous day and be grateful of what you did that day.
  • That to me is an ideal life.
There are very few intelligent people who stopped doing drugs, who is now in their 80s who encourages that life to others.
  • Because with great highs come great lows. Baseline dopamine.
  • But with low highs and low lows, the highs actually shine more prominently.
  • And you can then focus on building those highs, without the sacrifice of the lows.

Main takeaways

(Assuming that you have considered the prerequisites)
  • Is minimising sadness the right approach for you in particular? Will that approach give you your best life?
  • Instead of aiming for hapiness / joy / etc... Aim for less bad.
  • Think about how things affects long term negativity, however small.
  • Essentially, look at everything. Consider its value, how essential it is, how it affects your negativity score compared to alternatives, or even not having it, or changing it etc.
  • For any life decisions / choices you have to choose between, consider: Which choice is most likely to result in a future with a minimal negativity score?
  • For any purchases / things you need to get, consider: Which choice is most likely to result in a future with a minimal negativity score?
  • For every stage of your typical daily life/week/year, consider: Which choice is most likely to result in a future with a minimal negativity score?
  • Consider every item that you own. For each: Is that optimal for minimising my negavitiy? Is it essential to my life?
  • In every decision consider the affect on your lifetime negativity score
  • Analyse every part of your life; what you do, what you own, who you spend your time around etc. Optimise all for a lowest lifetime negativity score
