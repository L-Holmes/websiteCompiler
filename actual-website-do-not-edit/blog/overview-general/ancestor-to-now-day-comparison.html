IMPORTANT * This isn't the day of the life of a single tribe in Africa! *
it's a weighted average of what we predict our ancestors to have lived like
  • Habits/preferences done for longer are prioritised (e.g. if we and our ancestors both ate lettuce, that's good; things from 10,000 years ago not as reliable as 10,000,000 as we've not had as long with evolution...)
  • The more evidence the better
  • The more people that had those habits/preferences the better
  • Anything pre-"We're too resourceful to evolve [MAKE A BLOG ON THIS!!!]" ideally. We're letting evolution do the scientific tests and hypotheses for us. So after we evolved against it,.. less likely evolution did it's thing... [it's like people living with crippling disabilities in modern day but not dying...]
  • Still trying and stick to post apes though... as before then was just sooo different from us... but even still we can learn a lot even from many animals today...
  • Ignore anything we've clearly evolved away from (e.g. if our ancestors had massive jaws to crunch bones...)

A day in the life of our ancestors

A comparison of the day in the life of people living in africa before the great moving out — hunter gatherers waking up and seeing sun etc.

Awakening

Ancestor

  • Sun rises between 6am and 7am
  • Temp = 16c
  • Possibly after sleeping on a very primitive bed of leaves and mud.
  • Sleep together with other members for shared body heat and protection from predators.
  • Social time with other members: Mutual grooming to remove debris/parasites and low-volume vocalizing to check group status.

Modern Day

Morning

Ancestor

  • Await for rest of tribe to awake and get into the day. Around 30 mins.
  • Start walking towards water. Walk for 45 minutes on average (covering roughly 3km / 1.8 miles).
  • Get water around 1.5 hours after waking.
  • Social time with other members: Assessing the "freshness" of animal tracks near the water to decide the direction of the hunt.

Modern Day

Food

Ancestor

  • Go and hunt for food.
  • Catch and eat prey. Or alternatively start eating plants. Around 3 hours after waking.
  • Walk around 120 minutes in total during this (covering roughly 6km / 3.7 miles).
  • Run for around 5 minutes in total during this (most humans were not endurance based! Short sprints to corner small game).
  • Social time with other members: Cooperative foraging where individuals point out high-energy fruit or hidden tubers to the group.

Modern Day

Midday

Ancestor

  • Temp = 30c
  • Social time with other members: Napping in deep shade and "social pebble-play" or basic tool-sharpening while avoiding the midday heat.

Modern Day

Food, continued

Ancestor

  • Continue getting food
  • By now, mostly just getting veg and what not (tubers/berries) as active hunting becomes too thermically taxing in the heat.
  • Social time with other members: Sharing gathered plant-foods with those who were unsuccessful in the morning hunt.

Modern Day

Post eating

Ancestor

  • Stop eating around 6 hours after first meal. i.e. 9 hours after waking (approx 3:30 PM).
  • Social time with other members: Gathering "nesting" materials like fresh leaves or grass for the night's bedding while walking back to the home base.

Modern Day

Evening

Ancestor

  • Sun sets between 6:30pm and 7pm (around 12 hours of daylight)
  • Social time with other members: Sitting in a defensive circle, vocalizing "all-clear" signals, and reinforcing social bonds through touch before darkness.

Modern Day

Sleep

Ancestor

  • Sleep between 8:45pm and 9:45pm (assuming 2-3 hours after sundown, like modern hunter gatherers)
  • Temp = 21c
  • Social time with other members: Huddling in "clumps" for security; the high-oxytocin period where skin-to-skin contact lowers cortisol for sleep.
