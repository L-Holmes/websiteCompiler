

# intro

I hate the word "several".

Not dislike.

Not mildly inconvenient.

I hate it.

And I think we should genuinely consider… getting rid of it.


# setup

"But what's so bad about it?"

Right.

Let me take you back.


# personal story

Picture this.

Little Jimmy.

Back of the classroom.

Year 4.

Probably thinking about dinosaurs or something.

Teacher says:

"Draw several ducks."


Now.

Jimmy—being a logical, high IQ individual—thinks:

"Several… seven…"

Same vibe.

Same word.

Close enough.

So he draws 7 ducks.


Meanwhile.

Some absolute weapon across the room draws FOUR.

FOUR.

And apparently… he's correct.


So naturally, Jimmy raises his hand:

"Excuse me sire…"

"There must be seven… because it says several."


Wrong.

Completely wrong.

Publicly wrong.


The teacher doesn't just correct him.

No no.

She dismantles him.

Piece by piece.

Even hits him with a Google search just to finish the job.


For the next 3 days?

Jimmy is known for having:

"several breakfasts"

"several brain cells"

"several issues"


And honestly?

He couldn't give several clucks.


# turn

Now you might be thinking—

"Alright… but that's just Jimmy being a bit thick"


And yeah.

Fair.

But also…

WHY is the word like that?


# word origin

So I did some incredibly deep research.

By which I mean—

I spent about 5 minutes on Google.


Turns out…

"several" doesn't come from "seven" at all.

Shocking.


It comes from the Latin *separalis*

Meaning:

"separate"


So really…

It just means:

"a number… separated from others"

Which is basically just a posh way of saying:

"I don't know how many."


Brilliant.


# escalation / backstory

And here's the worst part.

This word has been around for AGES.


We're talking 1700s.

People like Thomas Paine—

you know, big revolutionary guy—

writing massively influential stuff…

using words like this.


His book *Rights of Man*?

Absolutely massive.

Like… stupidly big.


We're talking:

- tens of thousands of copies instantly  
- hundreds of thousands circulating  
- potentially millions over time  


And because people couldn't all read…

this stuff was read out loud.

In taverns.

To rooms full of people.


So somewhere—

in a loud British tavern—

some bloke is like:

"we must take action against several injustices—"


And everyone’s just nodding like:

"yeah mate… absolutely… several of them…"


No one knows how many.

No one asks.


# point

And that's the problem.


"Several" sounds precise.

But it’s not.

It’s completely useless.


It creates confusion.

Destroys clarity.

Ruins small children's lives.


# alternatives

Because we already have better words.


"a few"

"a handful"

"three"

"four"

Just… say the number.


Or if you don’t know—

say you don’t know.


Don’t hide behind "several" like a coward.


# bit

No one has EVER benefited from hearing:

"yeah just give me several minutes"

What is that?

3?

7?

45?


Just pick a number.

Stand by it.

Live with the consequences.


# conclusion

So here’s my proposal.

We retire the word.


We stop using it.

Immediately.


Let it fade into history.

Like floppy disks.

Or dignity.


And if you ever feel tempted to say it—

just remember Jimmy.


Sat at the back of the class.

Seven ducks.

Ruined life.


All because of "several".
