  • We are still guessing
  • Actual paleo doesn't work! It depends on the person's ancestors!
  • aka the Awpen diet...

Lose-weight dieting mistakes

WE ARE NOT EXPERTS
  • Not going for sustainability / focus on satiation

The microbiota approach

Have a primary aim to improve microbiota through eating natural foods that promote good bacteria to grow.
  • Diverse, natural, what our ancestors ate.

Key points

  • Our gut microbiota completely changes depending on what we eat!

Issues with low fodmap

  • Since you don't eat fodmaps, your gut doesn't grow the bacteria to deal with fodmaps! So any 'elimination' diet will always fail.
  • We know that beneficial bacteria like bifobacterium are good, but they only grow when we consume fodmaps like onions!

Possible theory on why I got ill

  • Eating bad food (Chinese, etc) upset my microbiome.
  • Since I have anxiety, that put me at a higher risk. (Hormones and puberty, life changes etc. intensified this)
  • Cutting out fodmaps and eating a high carb diet made things worse. My gut bacteria completely messed up
  • The IBS symptoms then made my anxiety and stress go through the roof.
  • My body learned to associate any irritation with issue. My gut has trauma.
Any thoughts on why a lot of people develop IBS straight after puberty finish?
Is it that individuals who have a genetic predisposition to anxiety, the hormones etc in puberty bring this out, and then after a traumatic gut event (e.g. food poisoning), their gut microbiota gets messed up, and then they can't recover because of the anxiety (which has come on since puberty)? and then the combination of IBS, anxiety and a 'bad gut' diet of low fibre white bread / cereals / sugar / rice etc. cause things to spiral cause they each affect each other, causing things to get worse?

The perfect storm

  • Vulnerability Established: Genetic predisposition to anxiety/stress sensitivity.
  • Vulnerability Activated: Puberty - hormones and brain maturation unmask anxiety/stress vulnerability.
  • Trigger: A gut infection (food poisoning) causes direct damage, inflammation, and dysbiosis.
  • Failure to Recover: Underlying anxiety/stress prevents full healing by maintaining gut dysfunction, inflammation, and dysbiosis via the gut-brain axis.
  • Symptom-Driven Diet: Gut symptoms lead to adoption of a low-fiber, high-refined-carb "safe" diet.
  • Spiral: The low-fiber diet starves good bacteria, worsens dysbiosis and inflammation, and further disrupts gut function. Ongoing gut symptoms fuel more anxiety/stress. Anxiety promotes poor dietary choices. The cycle intensifies, solidifying IBS.

Thus, fixing

  • Remove harmful carbs etc.
  • Slowly introduce other things, so my bacteria can build up without freaking out (like with a peanut allergy)
  • Eat probiotics etc. along the way.

To prevent for people

  • Avoid food poisoning / traumatic food events (i.e. no dodgy Chinese eating out)
  • Understand that anxiety may still cause IBS symptoms. Treat as needed, but then focus on keeping the gut fauna healthy. Perhaps eat less fodmaps only when about to have a very stressful event / just had one / are recovering. At all other times, slowly increase fodmaps.
  • Keep people informed. Let them make the choice and know the consequences.
