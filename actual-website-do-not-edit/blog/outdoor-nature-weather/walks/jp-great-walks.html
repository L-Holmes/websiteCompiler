## + - Links to when choosing a house / place to live... ## ### Some examples of great walks I've had which on-paper, wouldn't seem great. - relationship discussions in french forest - falling in ravine - falling in muddy puddles - falling in snow river, again ====== Order of importance when evaluating; - Company - Walk distance - Nature (no car/plane sounds! No annoying dogs/people, natural, little paving etc. ) - Walk variety, for less interesting stretches - Weather (either extreme weather or nice weather...) Just quite windy and quite on off rain annoying.. - Nice scenery... (rivers, lakes, waterfalls, hills, gorges...) Other unexpected bonuses - I enjoy walks with lots of other nice people having a good time. - Not honey pot sites with loads of tourists with phones - But people enjoying out hiking.
