HOW TO ADVERTISE

Principles

  • People want free easy cash

Tier 1

  • (No stakes, high rewards, high brand image, high number of people)
  • 'for the first month only'
  • We'll give you £5 for each person you convince to buy a shoe
    • Is this scummy?
  • 20% gift voucher

Tier 2

  • (No stakes, lower reward, low number of people)

Tier 3

  • (May loose money if no one likes it)
  • Convice local governments by saying that you'll have a charity box, and that 20% of sales will go to charity if they [let you setup a stall]
  • "We'll convince you that we're better than nike, or your money back!"
  • 50% off gift voucher for each person

Weird Tier

  • For every person you get to buy a [£1] flapjack with your code, we'll give you a free flapjack
    • (entice people with cheap, easy stuff)
  • If you buy a pair of shoes, then convince 2 more people, we'll give you £50
  • We'll give you 50p if you take our gift card and promise you'll convince people to look at it.
    • quite key Make them be workers / advertisers, by choice, but avoid the annoying minimum wage and legal things!!!!!!!!

    • e.g. avoid injury

      • e.g. a table, comparing the costs of resolving knee issues, buying lots of shoes etc. so they work out for themselves the better option

  • create another website, with:
    • decision calculators, that lead to my product
    • product comparison, that mentions my product as the best (but perhaps other products better at other non-functional things, like appearance)

Sponsor Requirements

  • All items they sell must be able to assist the user in some pursuit of minimising sadness
  • Items must meet all of the 'perfect item' criteria, for if I was making that product (given what is possible and available in the market)
  • company must be willing to go with my form of communication and contract (basic, no time wasted)
  • company must follow best non-annouing practices:
    • no cookies accept screen
    • excellent after sales support, where necessary
    • no data collection. Trivial to remove if so / if needed.
  • Items must be reasonably priced, given circumstances
  • items must lead the industry, when accounting for my requirements.
