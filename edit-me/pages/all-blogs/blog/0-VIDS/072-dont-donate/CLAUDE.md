Think about your three biggest struggles right now. Just quietly.

My guess — and I'm going off statistics here, not being dramatic —

it's something to do with health. Your body. Something you didn't choose.

Or a relationship. Family, usually. Something complicated and close.

How much is a YouTube subscription moving the needle on any of those?

That's not rhetorical. It's genuinely worth asking.

---

Small amounts are interesting though.

There's a reasonable argument that £5 is nothing — coffee, basically. Barely registers.

But there's another argument that £5 spent on the right thing at the right moment compounds in ways that are hard to predict.

A guy paid a fiver to get into a meetup he nearly skipped. Met someone. Built something. [find a real version of this]

The £5 that changes your life probably isn't the one that disappears into a tip jar.

It's the one that gets you somewhere you haven't been before.

Some numbers that don't get put next to each other often enough:

Average wait for an NHS therapist: **six weeks.** [verify]
Average wait for a psychiatrist: **up to 67 days.** [verify]
Average gap between developing mental health symptoms and receiving treatment: **eleven years.**

Global entertainment industry revenue: **£2.9 trillion.**

Neither of those things is anyone's fault. But they're both real, and they're happening at the same time.

Would the channel collapse if you stopped donating? Honestly — probably not.

None of this is about shaming anyone for supporting things they love. If something genuinely made a difference to you, tell people about it. Share it. That's worth more than money and it costs nothing.

It's more just — the donation model works partly because it creates a feeling of obligation. A sense that you owe something.

And most of the time, you don't.

---

Whatever you were going to spend — spend it on something closer to home. Something small. Something that might lead somewhere.

Then come back and tell me what happened. Genuinely.

Because that's the part I'm actually curious about.
