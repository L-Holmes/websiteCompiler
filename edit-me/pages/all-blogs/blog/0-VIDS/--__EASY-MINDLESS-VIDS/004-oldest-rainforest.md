
This forest shouldn’t exist.

Because it’s older than the Amazon.

Much older.

The Daintree Rainforest has been around for over 100 million years.

Which means when dinosaurs were still alive—

this place already looked familiar.

Same dense canopy. Same tangled vines. Same humidity thick enough to feel.

And somehow… it survived everything.

Asteroid impacts. Ice ages. Continental drift.

While most ecosystems reset—

this one just… continued.

But here’s the twist—

It’s not stable.

It’s constantly fighting itself.

Plants here don’t just compete for sunlight—they compete for survival in extreme conditions.

Some trees grow toxins to stop being eaten.

Others evolve to ignore those toxins entirely.

It’s an arms race that’s been running for millions of years.

And you can see it.

Leaves with spikes.
Roots that climb other trees.
Plants growing on plants growing on plants.

It’s layered.

Not just in height—but in time.

Because every part of this forest is a solution to a problem that existed long before humans ever showed up.

Which means when you walk through it—

you’re not just seeing nature.

You’re seeing history that never stopped.
