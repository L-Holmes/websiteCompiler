# Why the app can't work

## why don't work;

- people don’t actually want random meetups with strangers
- they want people they know, trust, or at least feel compatible with
- without that, they won’t join or will churn quickly

### user acquisition / density problem
- requires lots of users at same time to work
- early stage = empty pools → bad experience → churn
- harder than Meetup because it depends on real-time matching
- risk that it never reaches critical mass

### safety / bad people problem
- one bad experience can permanently lose a user
- ID verification doesn’t prevent bad behaviour
- banning is reactive, not preventative
- need trust, reputation, or familiarity systems

### infrastructure problem (cafes / spaces)
- good shared work/social spaces aren’t consistently available
- ideal would be large, free, accessible environments (like uni libraries)
- needs seating availability, low cost/free, transport access
- not realistic to replicate everywhere → limits scalability

### core problems
- pool auto-meet removes user control → increases hesitation
- too system-driven, ignores human behaviour
- too similar to Meetup without clear advantage
- too much friction in flow (steps, decisions, coordination)
- cafe ratings (wifi, noise, etc) distract from core goal

### what works
- real-time “i’m free now” signal
- small groups (3–4 people)
- familiar, repeatable environments

### key insight
- problem is reducing social risk, not coordinating meetups

### better direction
- show who’s free nearby instead of pools
- enable low-commitment meetups (coffee, walk, short cowork)
- add light filtering or shared context
- design for repeat interactions and familiarity

### blunt takeaway
- idea is too mechanical for a human problem
- needs shift from logistics → trust and comfort

### focus
- opt-in availability + activity-based matching is stronger direction
