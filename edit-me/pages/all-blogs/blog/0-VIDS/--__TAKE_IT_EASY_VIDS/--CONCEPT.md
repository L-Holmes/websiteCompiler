# what works: 
Landscapes & Aerials: Drone photography has saturated the stock market with incredible, cinematic views of the Earth.

Macro Textures: Close-ups of rust, wood, water droplets, or fabric. These are great for backgrounds while you overlay text or custom diagrams.

Food & Ingredients: High-end culinary photography is everywhere. (A niche about the "History of Spices" would look incredible).
Atmospheric & "Liminal" 

Photography: Empty streets, foggy mornings, dark alleys. These are perfect for setting a mood without requiring human subjects.

=====

# post processing
The Ken Burns Effect: Slowly pan and zoom on every single image. A static image is boring; a slowly moving image feels like a video.

Heavy Sound Design: If you show a stock image of a stormy coastline, the viewer must hear waves and wind. Sound design does 50% of the heavy lifting in bringing still images to life.

Fast Pacing: Don't linger on one photo for 15 seconds. Switch images every 3 to 5 seconds to keep visual momentum.
