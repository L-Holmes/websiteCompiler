# Music;

- Room tone / natural room ambience.
- Very subtle environmental bed, such as quiet wind, distant urban hush, or soft indoor air.
- Location-appropriate ambience, such as light birdsong, far traffic, distant water, or room acoustics.
- Gentle transitional sound effects for scene changes, text reveals, or section breaks.
- Soft object or action sounds that match what is being shown, such as page turns, marker taps, keyboard clicks, or footsteps.
- Sparse “life layer” sounds, used very lightly and only when the scene truly supports them.
- Selective natural sounds, like birds or water, only in outdoor or nature contexts.
- Silence, when the moment is important and you want maximum focus.


# Music / sound
Base layer = room tone (always consistent)
Add subtle environmental bed (very low)
    Wind / room / distant city
Add light context sounds when relevant
    Footsteps, page turns, etc.
Use transition sounds sparingly
    Section changes only
Use silence for emphasis
Decide upfront:
    No music (clean, serious)
    OR
    Very subtle music (controlled, consistent)
    Core rule (this is the bit people miss)

# Editing (condensed rules)
Never leave a static image dead
    Always slow zoom or pan (8–15s)
Match motion to narration
    Zoom in = focus
    Zoom out = context
    Pan = multiple elements
Cut based on ideas, not time
    New idea → new visual
Slow down for complex sections
    Especially diagrams (10–25s)
Use mostly hard cuts
    Fancy transitions = amateur signal
Vary motion between shots
    Repeating same zoom = robotic
Use silence deliberately
    Important point = less sound, not more
