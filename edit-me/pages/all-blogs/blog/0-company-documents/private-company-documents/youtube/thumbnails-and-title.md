# thumbnail
- stand out from rest (unique art style) 
- minimal. 
- add contrast in the image
- faces / text 
- should compliment, not mirror title. 
    (E.g. 42 hours... I survived quiet room... Hidden secrets with plane -> boarding at back).
    Proof subtly that you have niche insights ,without fully revealing why etc. 
- subtle highlights 
    (darkening, blurring, making smaller, no highlight etc. on certain parts) 
    (but not big obvious red arrows etc) 

------------
e.g. 


"How smart people do [X]"
 * [thumbnail]
 * 'No' on left, showing what you'd expect
 * 'Yes' on right showing the "better way"
   (i.e. subverting expectations)
"The lie we were all told about [X]"
 * [thumbnail]
 * Commonly accepted "fact" or image crossed out in red
 * Blurred or mysterious true image with a glowing outline or question mark
   (i.e. creating a knowledge gap)
"You only know 10% of [X]"
 * [thumbnail]
 * Tip of an iceberg above water labeled with a basic, well-known fact
 * Massive, dark underwater section labeled with a complex/obscure concept
   (i.e. visual representation of depth/complexity)
"Why [X] actually failed"
 * [thumbnail]
 * The popular entity/product looking pristine in the background
 * A massive, bold red graph crashing downwards in the foreground
   (i.e. stark visual contrast to a popular belief)
"The [X] rule that changes everything"
 * [thumbnail]
 * Complex, messy diagram on the left representing the "hard way"
 * Single, elegant glowing shape or icon on the right representing the "easy way"
   (i.e. promising a shortcut or simplification)
"How [X] went from [A] to [B]"
 * [thumbnail]
 * [Year 1] on the left with a primitive or failing example
 * [Year 2] on the right with an advanced or dominating example
   (i.e. highlighting a dramatic transformation)
"The dark economics of [X]"
 * [thumbnail]
 * Familiar logo or everyday item centered
 * Complex, chaotic spiderweb of money symbols or connections glowing behind it
   (i.e. revealing hidden systems)
"What happens when you combine [X] and [Y]?"
 * [thumbnail]
 * Object/Concept A on the left
 * Object/Concept B on the right
 * A bright, stylized explosion or glowing question mark in the middle
   (i.e. building extreme curiosity)
