
lighting

Backlight primarily

Match the exposure of the scene to the brightest light in the background

use a side/back artificial light, in the same colour temp to mimic the lighting of background on subjects face

don't have sun in shot (too bright)

use white screen to reflect sun onto faces

Scenery
Colour of background should contrast the subject
background should usually be visually interesting (not plain / minimal...)
Actor placement
Shots
use cinematic shots like over the shoulder etc.
use the rule of half (never go beyond the half way point)
Framing
rule of thirds (subjects at intersection point)
leading lines pointing to where to look
Camera settings
background blur

General:

use a soft source of light on actors face
film from the dark side of the face.
elevating

(Without too much time, or proprietary software)

Transitions:

whip pan (motion blur to other subject)
very fast zoom
audio from one scene bleeds into another (J-cut / L-cut)
something natural obscures the lens (e.g. someone walking in front of camera)

Editting:

slow movement on still photos
pseudo light leak (light around edge)
colour grade (more orange red)
